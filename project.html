My School Activities

A brief showcase of web projects highlighting layout, content and UX design.

Flower Shop

The website is a simple flower shop design that showcases different indoor plants with their names, pictures, and prices. It has clear sections like Home, About, Products, and Contact Us, making it easy to explore and understand what the shop offers. The goal of the site is to present the plants in a neat and visually pleasing way, while also giving visitors the basic information they need if they were interested in buying.

Culinary Adventure

The website Taste Of Travel is a food-blog style page inviting visitors to explore the bold flavors of Thai cuisine. It highlights a featured recipe — Tom Yum Goong — with detailed sections for ingredients, preparation steps, and cooking tips. Through descriptive text and visual cues (like an image of the Thai soup), it communicates not just how to cook a dish, but why its flavor components (sweet, sour, salty, spicy) matter. The site combines storytelling and culinary education to engage food lovers and home cooks.


Harmony Heights

The Harmony Heights site is a band-focused page that introduces the group, shares their music, and keeps fans in the loop. It includes a section about the band’s style and members, options to listen to their latest tracks, embedded performance clips, upcoming tour dates, social media follow-links, and a contact form. The overall aim is to connect with visitors via music and performance, making it easy for fans to engage and stay updated.

My First Webpage

The My First Webpage site is a personal introduction page that simply welcomes visitors with a clean headline “Welcome to my Webpage!” followed by a short note “This is my first webpage.” It’s minimalistic by design—just enough to demonstrate basic HTML skills and give a first impression of the author’s ability to put content online.


Form 101

The Form 101 page is a simple web form demo that invites users to fill in basic personal details (name, address, email, contact number), choose their gender, and select hobbies. It also includes some placeholder text (“Lorem ipsum …”) and a header image, which suggests this page is built to practice form layout, user input fields, and basic design structure.

Form 102

The Forms 102 page is a student record form designed to gather personal and academic details. It asks for information like full name, major, expected graduation year, address (with province selection), email, phone number, and preferred method of contact. It also requests the date of first school visit and a short self-description in three words. The layout focuses on collecting structured inputs in a clean, straightforward manner.
